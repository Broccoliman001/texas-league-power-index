How Power Score Is Calculated

The Texas League Power Index is a weighted analytical rating designed to measure overall team strength beyond simple win-loss record.

Rather than focusing only on standings, the model emphasizes sustainable performance indicators such as run differential, expected record, and performance against winning teams.

Power Score Components

  • 50% Run Differential Per Game
  • 25% Actual Winning Percentage
  • 25% Quality Record
    • 70% Winning Percentage vs >.500 Teams
    • 30% Share of Games Played vs >.500 Teams

Rating Behavior

Power Scores are designed to behave more like long-term team strength ratings than daily standings.

  • Ratings are smoothed over time using previous published scores.
  • Final displayed scores are compressed toward league average.
  • Trend arrows compare against weekly baseline snapshots.

Interpreting The Ratings

Small differences in Power Score are meaningful. Approximate tier ranges:

  • 65+ — Elite
  • 58–64 — Strong contender
  • 45–57 — Competitive middle tier
  • 35–44 — Weak
  • Below 35 — Poor

The model values how teams win — not just how often they win.
