How Power Score Is Calculated

The Texas League Power Index is a weighted analytical rating designed to measure overall team strength beyond simple win-loss record.

Rather than focusing only on standings, the model emphasizes sustainable performance indicators such as run differential, expected record, and performance against winning teams.

Power Score Components

  • 50% Run Profile
    • 60% Run Differential Per Game
    • 25% Expected Winning Percentage (X-W/L)
    • 7.5% Runs Scored Per Game
    • 7.5% Runs Allowed Per Game
  • 25% Actual Winning Percentage
  • 25% Quality Record
    • 70% Winning Percentage vs >.500 Teams
    • 30% Share of Games Played vs >.500 Teams

All categories are normalized against the rest of the league before the final score is calculated.

Rating Behavior

Power Scores are designed to behave more like long-term team strength ratings than daily standings.

  • Ratings are smoothed over time using previous published scores.
  • Final displayed scores are compressed toward league average to reduce unrealistic separation between teams.
  • Trend arrows and weekly movement compare against a baseline snapshot from the start of each baseball week.

Interpreting The Ratings

Small differences in Power Score are meaningful. Approximate tier ranges:

  • 65+ — Elite
  • 58–64 — Strong contender
  • 45–57 — League average / competitive middle tier
  • 35–44 — Weak
  • Below 35 — Poor

The model values how teams win — not just how often they win. Dominant run differential, strong expected records, and success against quality opponents are rewarded more heavily than narrow wins alone.
